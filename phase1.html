Phase 1

Resume

As you begin college and start to think about your career plans and “what’s next?” An important first step is to create a resume. Your resume is an important tool you will use during your job search as it showcases your skills, education, and accomplishments to your potential employers. Here’s where it can get a little scary… on average, recruiters spend 6 seconds on a resume (WOW). When I first heard this statistic I was very shocked as I realized it takes me longer to write my full name than it takes for a recruiter to read a resume.


Fear not! In this section, I will go over everything you need to make a solid resume!

Common Resume Mistakes To Avoid:

courtesy of a medium article linked Here


Not emphasizing your skills enough:

As recruiters have less than 10 seconds to review your resume, make sure to not bury your technicals skills but rather EMPHASIZE them!


Not detailing projects enough:

As you write about a role or project, make sure to emphasize the following:

~ What the role/project is

~ How you contributed

~ What technical skills you used (if applicable)

~ The impact of your role

Linkedin

Another important tool to be used during your job search is a website called Linkedin. You can think of it as a social media platform where you can network with professionals, furthering your career development. Linkedin can be used to display your experience, make connections, and search for jobs.
